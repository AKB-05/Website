Waterfall Bay

Pok Fu Lam · Pirate Massacre · Restless Waters

The Legend

During the Qing dynasty, ruthless pirates patrolled the waters around Hong Kong. Legend says that after a brutal raid, the pirates dumped the bodies of their victims into the bay at Waterfall Bay. Locals whisper that on stormy nights, you can still hear the desperate cries of the drowned echoing across the water. Fishermen avoid the area after dark, believing the restless spirits pull swimmers toward the depths. The rocky shore, once stained with blood, is said to be haunted by the souls who never found their way home.

Historical Background

Waterfall Bay was once a notorious hideout for pirates during the Qing dynasty. The bay's secluded coves and rocky coastline made it an ideal location for smuggling and illegal activities. Local records mention pirate attacks on fishing villages in the area during the 18th and 19th centuries. Today, the bay is known for its dramatic rock formations and is a popular spot for photography, but locals still share stories of ghostly sightings and unexplained sounds near the water after sunset.

Safety First
  • Do not swim in the bay - strong currents and sharp rocks
  • Check tide schedules before visiting (avoid high tide)
  • Stay on marked paths and public viewing areas
  • Visit during daylight hours only
  • Wear sturdy shoes for walking on rocky terrain
  • Download offline Google Maps for Pok Fu Lam area
  • Respect local residents and private property
