Safety Guide

Explore Hong Kong's haunted sites responsibly and safely

Before You Go

Download Offline Maps
Mobile signals can be weak near mountains, coastal areas, and remote islands. Download offline Google Maps before leaving. Google Maps Offline Guide →
Check Weather Forecast
Always check before visiting outdoor locations. Avoid typhoon signals, heavy rain, or extreme heat warnings. Hong Kong Observatory →
Wear Appropriate Footwear
Many locations have uneven terrain, rocky paths, or steep stairs. Wear sturdy, closed-toe shoes with good grip.
Bring Essentials
Carry plenty of water, sunscreen, insect repellent, a portable charger, and a small first-aid kit.

Location Safety

📍 Yim Tin Tsai (Sai Kung)

• Check ferry schedule before going - last ferry returns around 5:30pm
• No shops or restaurants on the island - bring your own water and snacks
• Wear sturdy shoes for uneven paths and rocky areas
• Stay on marked trails - some areas are overgrown or unstable
• Download offline map for Sai Kung area before departure

📍 Waterfall Bay (Pok Fu Lam)

• DO NOT SWIM - strong currents and sharp rocks make swimming extremely dangerous
• Check tide schedules before visiting - visit during low tide only
• Wear sturdy, non-slip shoes for walking on wet rocks
• Stay on marked coastal paths - some areas are slippery and unstable
• Visit during daylight hours only

📍 Nam Koo Terrace (Wan Chai)

• View from public street ONLY - the building is structurally unsafe
• Do not attempt to enter - illegal and dangerous
• Respect local residents - this is a residential area
• Best viewed during daylight hours
• Take photos from public pavement only

📍 High Street Haunted House (Sai Ying Pun)

• View from public footpath ONLY - the building is sealed and unstable
• Do not attempt to enter - extreme danger, structural collapse risk
• Respect the historical trauma associated with this site
• Visit during daylight for best viewing and safety
• Photography allowed from public areas only

Respectful Tourism

Historical Trauma
These locations have witnessed suffering, death, and tragedy. Visit with awareness and respect for those who suffered here.
Do Not Disturb
Do not leave trash, graffiti, or any markings. Leave no trace. Do not disturb any memorials, offerings, or religious items.
Quiet Reflection
Keep noise levels low. These are somber locations, not entertainment venues. Be mindful of local residents living nearby.
Photography Etiquette
Photography is allowed from public areas only. Do not use flash inside or near any memorial sites. Be discreet and respectful.
