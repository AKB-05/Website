Nam Koo Terrace

Wan Chai · Wartime Ghosts · 1918 Mansion · Comfort Women Spirits

The Legend

Built in 1918, this red-brick mansion hides a dark past. During the Japanese occupation of Hong Kong in WWII, Nam Koo Terrace was allegedly used as a brothel for comfort women. Locals say the spirits of the women who suffered and died here still roam the halls. Visitors have reported hearing soft crying, seeing shadowy figures at the windows, and feeling an overwhelming sense of sadness. The mansion has been abandoned for decades, but on quiet nights, neighbours claim to see candlelight flickering in the upper floors where no one has lived for years.

Historical Background

Nam Koo Terrace is a historic red-brick mansion built in 1918 in Wan Chai. During the Japanese occupation of Hong Kong (1941-1945), the building was allegedly used as a brothel for comfort women. The building has been vacant for decades and is now a recognised haunted site in Hong Kong. Despite its dark history, the architecture remains a fine example of early 20th-century colonial style. The building is currently under conservation consideration and can only be viewed from the public street.

Safety First
  • View from public street only - do not attempt to enter
  • The building is structurally unstable and off-limits
  • Visit during daylight hours for best viewing
  • Respect local residents and the sensitive history of the site
  • Take photos from public pavement only
  • Do not disturb any makeshift memorials or offerings
