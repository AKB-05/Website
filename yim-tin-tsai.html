Yim Tin Tsai

Sai Kung · Ghost Island · Abandoned Church · Midnight Bells

The Legend

Locals say the abandoned St. Joseph's Chapel bells ring at midnight, even though the ropes were cut decades ago. Some visitors report hearing children singing from the ruined schoolhouse. Whether ghost or guilt, Yim Tin Tsai holds secrets the salt fields never washed away. The Hakka village, abandoned in the 1990s, is said to be watched over by the spirits of its former residents who refuse to leave their ancestral home.

Historical Background

Yim Tin Tsai means "Little Salt Pan" in Chinese. It was the only salt production location in Hong Kong. The island was settled by Hakka people in the 19th century and once had 500 residents. St. Joseph's Chapel, built in 1890, is a Grade III historic building and won a UNESCO Asia-Pacific Heritage Award in 2005. The village was completely abandoned in the 1990s but has seen conservation efforts since 2011.

Safety First
  • View from public ferry and marked paths only
  • Do not enter abandoned buildings or the old school
  • Check ferry schedule before going (last ferry returns before 6pm)
  • Bring water, sunscreen, and mosquito repellent
  • Respect local beliefs and the Catholic heritage of the island
  • Download offline Google Maps for Sai Kung area before departure
