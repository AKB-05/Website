High Street Haunted House

Sai Ying Pun · Former British Asylum · Plague Hospital · Haunted Halls

The Legend

Built in 1892 as the first mental hospital in Hong Kong, this imposing colonial building has a dark and tragic history. During the bubonic plague outbreak, the building was converted into a plague hospital where hundreds died in agony. Locals say the restless spirits of former patients still wander the corridors. Visitors report hearing disembodied screams, seeing faces pressed against the sealed windows, and feeling icy cold spots. The building has been vacant for decades, but on foggy nights, some claim to see ghostly figures in old-fashioned asylum gowns standing at the windows, staring out at the living.

Historical Background

The High Street Haunted House was built in 1892 as the first mental hospital in Hong Kong. During the bubonic plague outbreak in 1894, the building was converted into a plague hospital where hundreds of patients died. After the plague subsided, it continued as a mental hospital until 1961. The building has been vacant since the 1970s and is now a declared monument. It is widely considered one of the most haunted locations in Hong Kong, with numerous reported sightings and paranormal experiences from passersby over the decades.

Safety First
  • View from public street and footpath only - building is sealed and off-limits
  • Do not attempt to enter - the structure is unstable and dangerous
  • Visit during daylight hours for best viewing and safety
  • Respect local residents and the historical significance of the site
  • Photography is allowed from public areas only
  • Be respectful - this was once a place of suffering and death
